I was born in Jacksonville, raised in Callahan, and then came back home to Jacksonville FL. I've taken many detours in life and have become a better man for it. You can catch me at a Jags game or reading a book on the beach. At home I can be found tinkering with a Breadboard and Arduino or working on various pet projects such as this webpage. The Man Who Made Me

Expectations

October 12th 2018 is a day that will forever live in my memory. That day my father suddenly passed away. Jeffrey Wayne Evans wore many hats in his life from his days as a Volunteer Firefighter becoming the Lieutenant of Station 8 to creating and running his own Small Business (Jeffery's Attic) while still working a full time job. His work ethic was unquestionable and my father left huge shoes to fill. This started my path in writing as an outlet to understand and come to terms with our loss. My poem Expectaions was written for my father and can be read here via Wattpad.

Soul Power

Music On My
Mind

Music has been a part of my life since playing the Tuba in middleschool and has taken me places ever since. In highschool I transitioned to Drumline and used one of my summers to participate in the DCI World Championships playing at Lucas Oil Stadium of the Indianapolis Colts. Playing Tuba in college helped me pay for my Associates degree at Florida State College at Jacksonville and I even played for Jacksonville University's drumline for a short period. Music helped me wander. And wandering helped me experience life in ways I will forever appreciate.
